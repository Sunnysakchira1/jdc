Window Tinting Cost in Thailand:
What You'll Actually Pay

Exact price ranges by film type, property size estimates for condos and villas, and honest ROI calculations.

6 min read Updated June 2025 By JaiDeeClear

Window tinting in Thailand is priced per square foot of glass, not per window or per room. Understanding this — and knowing the typical glass area for different property types — lets you estimate your total cost before requesting a quote.

Prices vary based on film tier, glass area, accessibility, and whether old film needs to be removed. Here's a complete breakdown.

What Affects the Price

1. Film Tier

The biggest variable. Sputtering film costs 2–3× more per sqft than Carbon. For most residential properties, Ceramic Nano is the sweet spot — meaningful performance improvement over Carbon with a manageable price premium.

2. Glass Accessibility

Ground and lower floor glass is straightforward. High-floor exterior glass requiring scaffolding, elevated platforms, or rope access adds 20–40% to the installation cost. Interior glass (which is the case for most condos and homes) rarely has access issues.

3. Old Film Removal

If your glass already has tint that needs replacing, film removal adds ฿20–30/sqft. This is necessary when old film is bubbling, peeling, or discoloured — you cannot install new film over old.

4. Special Glass Types

Double-glazed (IGU) windows, laminated safety glass, and curved glass require specific film grades and more careful installation. We assess glass type during the free measurement and factor this into the quote.

5. Property Location

We serve all major Thai cities — Bangkok, Phuket, Pattaya, Hua Hin, Koh Samui, Krabi, and Chiang Rai — with no travel surcharge within city limits.

ROI: When Does Window Tinting Pay for Itself?

The financial case for window tinting is strongest in Thailand because of high electricity costs and extreme solar gain. Ceramic Nano film typically reduces AC energy consumption by 20–35% in exposed glass properties.

Is Cheap Film Worth It?

The window film market in Thailand includes unbranded products sold at ฿25–40/sqft. These films typically use dye-based technology that fades within 12–18 months in Thailand's UV environment — turning purple, bubbling, and peeling. The short warranty (if any) means you pay for replacement within 2 years.

Our Carbon Series at ฿50–70/sqft uses premium carbon-based film with a 2-year manufacturer warranty. It's the minimum we install — because it's the minimum that holds up in Thailand's climate. For most properties, the incremental cost of stepping up to Ceramic Nano is small relative to the performance gain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there any hidden charges?

No. The price we quote after on-site measurement is final. Installation, glass cleaning, and warranty documentation are all included. The only additional charge that can arise is film removal if you have old film that needs to come off — and we flag this during the measurement visit before you commit.

Do you offer discounts for large properties or multiple units?

Yes. For villa complexes, apartment blocks, or commercial properties with substantial glass area, we provide project pricing. Contact us by WhatsApp with your property details and we'll put together a package quote.

Can I choose different films for different rooms?

Absolutely. It's common to use Ceramic Nano on main living areas and sea-view bedrooms, Carbon on utility rooms and north-facing glass. We help you optimise the mix during the on-site consultation.
