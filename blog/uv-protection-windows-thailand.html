UV Protection

Thailand UV Index: 14.
Your Glass Blocks Almost None of It.

What UV actually does to your health, flooring, and furniture — and why window film is the only effective solution.

5 min read Updated June 2025 By JaiDeeClear

Most people move to Thailand aware of the heat. Fewer think about UV radiation — which is both more pervasive and more damaging to a property's interiors and occupants' health than heat alone.

Thailand sits at 5–20°N latitude. This proximity to the equator means the sun's angle is steep year-round, shortening the path through the atmosphere and maximising UV intensity at ground level. A UV index of 14 isn't rare — it's a regular summer afternoon in Bangkok, Phuket, or any Thai coastal city.

UVA vs UVB: What Actually Gets Through Your Windows

UV radiation is divided into two categories relevant to windows:

  • UVB (280–315nm) — the radiation responsible for sunburn. Standard glass blocks most UVB effectively. This is why you don't burn sitting by a closed window — but it gives a false sense of protection.
  • UVA (315–400nm) — longer wavelength, penetrates deeper, causes 10× more exposure than UVB. Standard clear glass transmits 70–80% of UVA. This is the radiation that fades flooring, ages skin, and accumulates silently over years of indoor sun exposure.

The important nuance: Not burning behind glass doesn't mean you're protected from UV. UVA penetrates standard glass freely — and UVA is responsible for photoageing, immune suppression, and is strongly implicated in skin cancer development through cumulative exposure.

What UV Does to Your Property

🪵

Hardwood Floors

UV bleaches and greys hardwood within 6–18 months of direct sun exposure in Thailand. Teak, oak, and engineered flooring are all vulnerable. The damage is cumulative and irreversible without full replacement.

🛋️

Furniture & Textiles

Silk, cotton, and dyed synthetics fade noticeably within 12 months. Leather dries and cracks. UV degradation of textiles occurs even in indirect sunlight exposure — reflected UV from bright surfaces is sufficient.

🎨

Art & Valuables

Paintings, prints, and photographs are highly UV-sensitive. Professional gallery standards call for UV-filtered glass to protect art. Without film, artworks near windows degrade rapidly under Thailand's UV.

💊

Health & Skin

People who work or spend extended time near windows accumulate UVA exposure equivalent to outdoor exposure. Cumulative UVA is the primary driver of photoageing and plays a significant role in melanoma risk.

How Window Film Blocks UV

Quality window film uses one of two technologies to block UV radiation:

Nano-Ceramic Particles

Ceramic films embed nano-scale particles — typically titanium dioxide, indium tin oxide, or antimony tin oxide — within the film matrix. These particles absorb UV photons, converting the UV energy to infrared heat which is then dissipated through the glass. The result is 99% UV blocking without significantly reducing visible light transmission.

Metal Oxide Sputtering

Sputtering films deposit metal oxide layers at the atomic scale using a vacuum magnetron process. These layers reflect UV at the molecular level — comparable to how professional anti-reflective coatings work in optics. Sputtering films achieve the highest UV rejection across the broadest spectrum.

Why UV400 Film Matters in Thailand

Standard window films are certified to block UV up to 380nm. This covers most of the UV spectrum — but leaves a narrow UVA window (380–400nm) unprotected. This gap is small but meaningful: 380–400nm UVA is particularly deeply penetrating and responsible for a disproportionate share of skin ageing effects.

UV400 film extends protection to 400nm — covering the complete UV spectrum including this final UVA window. For properties where occupants spend significant time near glass (home offices, living rooms, bedrooms), UV400 provides complete protection beyond what standard UV-blocking films deliver.

Monsoon Season: Does UV Film Still Work?

Yes. UV radiation penetrates cloud cover effectively. On an overcast day in Thailand, UV index still reaches 6–8 — well within the "high" category. The mistaken belief that cloud cover means UV protection is one of the most common misconceptions about UV exposure.

Window film provides consistent UV blocking year-round, whether the sky is clear or overcast. This matters particularly for Phuket and Krabi properties during the May–October monsoon season, when owners might assume reduced UV risk.

What About Low-E Glass?

Low-E (low-emissivity) glass is common in newer Thai condominiums and luxury developments. It has a metallic coating that reduces heat transfer through radiation. Low-E glass does provide some UV protection — typically 30–60% depending on the coating specification — but falls well short of the 99% blocking provided by window film.

Window film can be applied over Low-E glass to bring UV blocking to 99%. We recommend a low-expansion, low-absorption film grade for Low-E glazing to prevent thermal stress issues — our team assesses this during the free site visit.

Practical Guide: Which Film for UV Protection

All four of our film tiers block 99% UV. The distinction is in:

  • UV400 coverage — Ceramic UV400 and Sputtering Series cover the full 0–400nm UV spectrum. Carbon and standard Ceramic Nano cover up to ~380nm.
  • Heat rejection combined — UV and heat are related concerns. If you want UV protection with maximum heat rejection, Sputtering Series addresses both.
  • Light transmission — higher VLT films let more light through while still blocking UV. A 70% VLT Ceramic Nano film looks nearly clear while blocking 99% of UV.

For most UV-focused applications — flooring preservation, health protection, artwork care — Ceramic Nano at 65–70% VLT delivers the required protection at a reasonable cost.
