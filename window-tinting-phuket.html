Before You Book

Phuket window film — your questions answered.

How much does window tinting cost in Phuket?+
Window tinting in Phuket runs from ฿70/sq ft for carbon film up to ฿180/sq ft for premium sputtering. A typical 3-bedroom villa lands around ฿15,000–45,000 depending on glass area and film tier. We confirm the exact figure free during an on-site measurement.
Do you cover all areas of Phuket island?+
Yes — we serve the entire island including Patong, Kata, Karon, Kamala, Bang Tao, Cherng Talay, Rawai, Chalong, Phuket Town, Kathu, Surin, Layan and Cape Panwa. As a Phuket-based team, our technicians travel to your villa, condo or resort for a free measurement and on-site installation.
How long does window film installation take?+
A typical Phuket condo takes 3–5 hours. A 3–4 bedroom villa takes about one full day. Larger resort or commercial projects take 1–3 days. You can stay in the property during installation.
Which window film do you recommend for Phuket?+
For most Phuket villas and sea-view condos, Ceramic Nano offers the best balance of heat rejection, UV protection and clarity — it blocks intense coastal UV while keeping the Andaman Sea view crystal clear. For large west-facing villa glass in Kamala or Rawai, Ceramic UV400 adds extended UV blocking. For luxury villas and resorts, our Sputtering Series delivers maximum performance.
How much will I save on electricity with window film?+
Most Phuket customers see a 20–30% reduction in monthly electricity bills. Villas with large glass areas and heavy A/C usage see the biggest drop — some owners in Rawai and Kamala report saving ฿2,000–4,000+ per month during hot season.
Is window film effective during Phuket's monsoon season?+
Yes. Film provides year-round benefits — it blocks solar heat during Phuket's hot season (February–May) and continues blocking UV even on overcast monsoon days (May–October). UV rays penetrate cloud cover, so protection is consistent regardless of weather. Safety films also hold glass in place during monsoon storms.
