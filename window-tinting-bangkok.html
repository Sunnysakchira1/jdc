Common Questions About Window Tinting in Bangkok

How much does window tinting cost in Bangkok? +

Window tinting in Bangkok ranges from \u0E3F50-70/sq.ft for carbon film to \u0E3F155+/sq.ft for premium sputtering film. A typical 2-bedroom condo costs \u0E3F5,400-11,000 depending on glass area and film choice. We provide free on-site measurements and quotes.

Do you cover all areas of Bangkok? +

Yes ‚Äî we serve all Bangkok districts including Sukhumvit, Silom, Sathorn, Ladprao, Bangna, Thonburi, Asok, Ratchada, Chatuchak, and surrounding areas like Nonthaburi. Our technicians travel to your property for free measurement and installation.

How long does window film installation take? +

A typical condo takes 3-5 hours. A townhouse takes one full day. Larger commercial projects or office buildings take 1-3 days. You can stay in the property during installation.

Which window film do you recommend for Bangkok condos? +

For most Bangkok condos, our Ceramic Nano film offers the best balance of heat rejection, UV protection, and clarity ‚Äî it maintains city views while blocking intense urban heat. For west-facing units, Ceramic UV400 provides extended UV spectrum blocking. For offices and commercial, our Sputtering Series delivers maximum performance.

How much will I save on electricity with window film? +

Most Bangkok customers see a 20-30% reduction in monthly electricity bills. High-rise condos with heavy AC usage see the biggest savings ‚Äî some condo owners in Sukhumvit and Silom report saving \u0E3F1,500-3,000+ per month during hot season.

Does window film block natural light? +

Our Ceramic Nano and Ceramic UV400 films maintain high visible light transmission ‚Äî rooms stay bright and natural-looking. You'll notice a dramatic reduction in heat and glare without losing light quality. This is especially important for Bangkok condos where natural light is a premium.

What's the warranty on your window film? +

All films carry a manufacturer-backed warranty: 2 years for Carbon Series, 5 years for Ceramic Nano and Ceramic UV400, and 7 years for Sputtering Series. Covers peeling, bubbling, discoloration, and delamination.

Can condo management approve window film installation? +

Window film is installed on the interior surface of glass, so it typically does not require juristic office approval. However, we recommend checking with your building management. We can provide technical specifications and samples to help with any approval process.

Can window film be removed or replaced? +

Yes ‚Äî window film is fully removable. If you want to upgrade or replace end-of-life film, we strip the old film and install new. Old film removal costs \u0E3F20-30/sq.ft additional.
