Before You Book

Bangkok window film — your questions answered.

How much does window tinting cost in Bangkok?+
Window tinting in Bangkok runs from ฿70/sq ft for carbon film up to ฿180/sq ft for premium sputtering. A typical 2-bedroom condo lands around ฿5,400–11,000 depending on glass area and film tier. We confirm the exact figure free during an on-site measurement.
Do you cover all areas of Bangkok?+
Yes — we serve every Bangkok district including Sukhumvit, Silom, Sathorn, Ladprao, Bangna, Thonburi, Asok, Ratchada, Chatuchak and surrounding areas. Our technicians travel to your condo, office or townhouse for a free measurement and on-site installation.
How long does window film installation take?+
A typical Bangkok condo takes 3–5 hours. A townhouse takes about one full day. Larger commercial projects or office floors take 1–3 days. You can stay in the property during installation.
Which window film do you recommend for Bangkok condos?+
For most Bangkok high-rise condos, Ceramic Nano offers the best balance of heat rejection, UV protection and clarity — it blocks intense urban heat while keeping panoramic city views crystal clear. For west-facing river units, Ceramic UV400 adds extended UV blocking. For CBD offices, our Sputtering Series delivers maximum performance.
How much will I save on electricity with window film?+
Most Bangkok customers see a 20–30% reduction in monthly electricity bills. High-rise condos with heavy A/C usage see the biggest drop — some owners in Sukhumvit and Silom report saving ฿1,500–3,000+ per month during hot season.
Can condo management approve window film installation?+
Window film is applied to the interior surface of the glass, so it typically does not require building management approval. We still recommend checking with your building management, and we can provide technical specifications and samples to help with any approval process.
